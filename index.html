Key Message: Empowering teams through accessible design systems

Situation: At Cosgrove Leisure, the sales team struggled to create on-brand marketing materials due to limited design skills.

Task: I needed to develop a solution that ensured brand consistency in sales materials while being easy for non-designers to use.

Action: I designed user-friendly overlay templates for Canva and PowerPoint, incorporating our brand colors, fonts, and layouts. I also created a guide on using these templates and held training sessions for the sales team.

Result: Achieved brand consistency across all sales materials, reduced the design team's workload by 40%, and empowered the sales team to create professional content.

Key Message: Strategic transformation with limited resources

Situation: When I joined Cosgrove Leisure, we had multiple outdated websites with poor user experience, slow load times, and poor lead generation.

Task: My task was to improve our web presence and lead generation within a limited budget.

Action: I implemented a WordPress solution using Elementor and the Houzez theme. I integrated our CRM, created an easy-to-use listing management system, added a booking system, and consolidated all parks under one website.

Result: Significantly improved our search engine rankings, increased lead generation, and boosted sales. Achieved a more professional online presence, comparable to larger competitors, despite budget constraints.

Key Message: Adaptive project management for maximum efficiency

Situation: At Cosgrove Leisure, I managed several digital projects simultaneously, including a website redesign, branding updates, and creating marketing collateral. The challenge was compounded by limited digital infrastructure and communication systems.

Task: I needed to coordinate these projects efficiently, keep stakeholders aligned, and meet tight deadlines.

Action: I used Trello for visual project management, adopted an Agile-like approach with sprints and deadlines, and used WhatsApp for communication. I also established regular milestone reviews.

Result: Led to more streamlined workflows, fewer revision cycles, improved stakeholder engagement, and on-time delivery of all major projects, including the website revamp.

Key Message: Making design tangible through interactive prototyping

Situation: When presenting creative concepts for landing pages to non-design stakeholders, static mockups often led to misunderstandings and lengthy revisions.

Task: I aimed to improve how we visualized concepts to stakeholders, streamline the feedback process, and reduce wasted development time.

Action: I started creating quick interactive prototypes using the Relume Library. This allowed stakeholders to interact with the designs early on. I also implemented early prototype reviews and facilitated hands-on demonstrations.

Result: Resulted in faster revision cycles, a quicker approval process, and better stakeholder buy-in, as the interactive prototypes made the design concepts much clearer.

Interview Strategy Tips

Preparation Checklist

  • Review each STAR response thoroughly and practice out loud.
  • Practice delivering responses within 2-3 minutes.
  • Prepare specific metrics and numbers to quantify your impact.
  • Have additional examples ready for follow-up questions.
  • Research PSI Services and Megan Gaffney thoroughly.
  • Prepare thoughtful questions to ask Megan.

Response Framework (STAR Method)

  1. Situation: Briefly describe the context.
  2. Task: Explain your responsibility.
  3. Action: Detail the steps you took.
  4. Result: Share the outcomes (quantify them!).

Key Competencies to Highlight

  • Strategic Thinking: Emphasize planning, especially in relation to website strategy.
  • Resource Optimization: Showcase using resources effectively in web projects.
  • Team Empowerment: Highlight enabling others in design and content creation.
  • Stakeholder Management: Demonstrate communication with stakeholders, including web managers.
  • Technical Adaptability: Show willingness to learn new web design and digital marketing technologies.
  • Results-Driven Approach: Focus on the impact of your work, especially on website performance.

Follow-up Questions to Prepare For

  • What's your approach to working within tight budgets on web projects?
  • How do you prioritize competing deadlines in website updates or redesigns?
  • What's your process for evaluating the effectiveness of your web design work?
